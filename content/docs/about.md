## About


**Principles of Data Engineering** is a practical manual on building data systems that produce reliable business insight.

The central premise of the book is:

> Data engineering is the process of applying business interest to data to create information, and then insight.

The role of the data engineer is therefore not just to move or transform data, but to shape it into a form that reflects real business entities, processes, and decisions.


## What this book focuses on

This book is written from delivery experience and focuses on patterns that hold up in real systems.

Key areas include:

- **Expressiveness** — ensuring data models correspond clearly to real-world business processes  
- **Fragment modelling** — building reusable, modular blocks of information rather than one-off outputs  
- **Entity processing and entity tracking** — the two core patterns for modelling immutable and mutable business entities  
- **Pipeline design** — the three-pass structure of *Filter → Map → Reduce* for building robust transformations  
- **Reference data and integration** — using reference tables to create clarity and consistency across systems  
- **Data quality and reliability** — tests, assumptions, and fault tolerance  
- **Efficient and stable pipelines** — load mechanics, dependencies, and change handling  

The emphasis throughout is on **clarity, reuse, and long-term maintainability**, rather than short-term outputs.


## Intended audience

This book is written for practitioners who are:

- Building or scaling data pipelines  
- Designing data warehouses or analytical models  
- Working with tools such as SQL, Power BI, or similar platforms  
- Responsible for turning raw data into reliable information for decision-making  

## How to read this book

The material can be read in two ways:

- **Sequentially** — to understand the full approach to data engineering  
- **By section** — to apply specific patterns (e.g. entity processing, dimensional modelling, pipeline design)  

The concepts are designed to be applied directly in production systems.


## Origin and release

This book was first written to support data engineers in the Australian Public Service and was released by the Department of Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry under Freedom of Information.

It is now made available for broader use.
